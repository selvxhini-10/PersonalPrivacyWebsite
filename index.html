What is Personal Privacy?

Personal privacy refers to the fundamental human right and ability to control and protect one’s personal information and data. As stated in a Harvard Law Review article "The Right to Privacy”, privacy can be defined as “the right to be let alone." People should be able to determine the extent to which their personal information is collected, processed and used by others. Personal data includes any confidential and sensitive information that can be used to identify an individual such as credit card numbers, social security numbers, health records and other identifying information. Privacy ensures this information is kept secure from unauthorized access.

Read About Facebook's Data Breach 2019

In 2019, over 530 million people’s personal Facebook data, including phone numbers, names, locations and email addresses were leaked. This leaked user profile data represented users in 106 countries and were posted to a hacker forum after malicious actors exploited a vulnerability in the system. While this data leak may seem trivial, users became increasingly vulnerable as a result.

The Impacts


Information technology poses a greater risk to personal privacy today than ever before. Advancements in generative artificial intelligence, data tracking and collection and mass electronic surveillance and spyware can lead to unauthorized access to confidential personal information. Corporations and government agencies regularly collect and process personal information and applications and websites track data and browsing habits to provide personalized user experiences and products. The detrimental impacts of these technologies and automated systems for storing and collecting information, from sensor networks, tracking cookies, social media, facial recognition technology, search engines, big data and technology to government cybersecurity programs lead to numerous unprecedented moral, social, political and legal issues in society.

Why is Personal Privacy Important?

In the age of modern computing, the protection of data and disclosure of personal information such as financial information and phone numbers continues to be imperative issues as they can be compromised in data breaches, cybercrimes, electronic intercepts, identity theft, fraud and more. People should be aware of these intrusions, and mandate boundaries regarding their preferences and data collection. Personal privacy is a fundamental human right that is necessary to ensure people’s personal autonomy, freedom of expression, integrity and social safety. The responsible and ethical usage of technology is imperative to prevent the misuse of personal identity and autonomy.


Personal data continues to be a valuable commodity in today’s market.
